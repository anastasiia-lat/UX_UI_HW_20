Oregon and Washington States trip

From untouched nature to modern cities

Nature Sightseeings

Amazing nature of the Pacific Northwest

Olympic National Park and Hoh Forest

Lush rainforests, snow-capped mountain peaks, and a rugged coast ripped straight from those scenic calendars hanging in the break room—Olympic National Park deserves at least one encounter in a lifetime. The Hoh Rainforest gets at least 12 feet of rain a year and is a unique temperate rainforest with a fascinating ecosystem.

Oregon waterfalls

In fact, the Columbia River Gorge in Northern Oregon is home to over 90 waterfalls alone. There are more waterfalls in Oregon than any other state. My favorites are: Multnomah Falls, Watson Falls, and Bridal Veil Falls.

Lakes - the best viewpoints of peaks

Lake Siskiyou and Trillium lake - reservoirs of incredible beauty are also the best viewing points for viewing mountain peaks. Crystal clear water, dense forest around, birds singing and snow-capped peaks in the distance - the best place to relax.

Food adventure

Portland Coffee

TPortland now has one of the richest coffee landscapes in the country. Portland's coffee culture has kept locals caffeinated with a mix of independent roasters and small cafés, each brewing up espressos, Americanos and lattes with beans from around the world.

Must try in Seattle

From fresh oysters to salmon, Seattle is a seafood town. Among the desserts it is worth noting the Dark Decadence cake, made up of deep, velvety Theo chocolate and dry-burned caramel, served alongside vanilla ice cream that's topped with nib toffee.

Big cities exploration

Seattle

Seattle is diverse, green, tasty, atmospheric and memorable. Boat trips, downtown exploration, numerous parks, gastronomic delights and architectural sights - I thoroughly enjoyed all this on a trip. But sunny weather played a big role in this.

Portland

Portland is a city that can be walked in half a day, with very few attractions and entertainment. so it's definitely not worth staying here for a long time on a journey.

Driving 1 Highway

Leave as early as you can

The road is winding, with speed limits and with a lot of stops - lookout points, so it takes a very long time. It is best to move out very early in the morning so that there is enough time to enjoy the views and take your time to go to the next overnight stop.

Have enough time to enjoy incredible views

The road and the landscapes around are incredibly picturesque and you want to stop and admire for a very long time, so it’s worth laying enough time for this.

Prepare enough food and drinks

It is better to enjoy the road and the sights on a full stomach. So be sure to have snacks and plenty of water with you.
